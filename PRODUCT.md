# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: COO / Head of Operations at crypto-adjacent companies (exchanges, DeFi platforms, crypto payment processors, fintechs, iGaming operators). They land here under pressure to scale customer support without hiring, often after an incident or growth spike exposed the limits of their in-house team. They are evaluating whether outsourcing is safe — they've been burned by (or heard horror stories about) generic BPOs that don't understand wallets, chain reorgs, KYC edge cases, or fraud patterns specific to crypto.

Secondary: technical stakeholders (CTO, Head of Risk/Compliance) pulled into the evaluation, and founders at earlier-stage platforms making the build-vs-buy support decision.

Job to be done: confirm quickly that Toeshee actually understands crypto operations (not a relabeled call centre), see proof it works (case studies, metrics), and request a technical evaluation with confidence.

## Product Purpose

Toeshee is a crypto-native customer support operations provider — "the first outsourcing provider built for the decentralized economy." The site exists to convert operations leaders into qualified evaluation requests. Success = a visitor leaves convinced that Toeshee is the steady, expert ops partner who will just handle it, and books a technical evaluation.

## Brand Personality

**Expert · Reliable · Low-drama.**

The ops partner that just handles it. Competence without ego. Calm authority over excitement: specific operational language (incident resolution, fraud/KYC exceptions, compliance at scale, SLAs) over marketing superlatives. Visitors should leave feeling steady and de-risked — the anxiety they arrived with ("can anyone external really handle our support?") replaced by quiet confidence.

Voice: precise, concrete, operational. Show metrics from real deployments, not aspirational claims. Never breathless, never hype.

## Anti-references

- **Generic BPO / call-centre sites**: stock-photo smiling headset agents, corporate blue, "we deliver excellence" copy. The opposite of crypto-native; instantly signals "they don't get it."
- **Hype-heavy Web3 / NFT aesthetics**: dark mode with neon gradients, spinning 3D tokens, DAO-project energy. Too speculative for a firm selling reliability.
- **Fintech "trustworthiness" clichés**: navy + gold, shield icons, "bank-grade security" cosplay. Legacy-finance signaling undercuts crypto credibility.

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over promises.** Real deployment metrics (−54% resolution time, 96% SL), named case studies, and concrete operational scenarios do the persuading. Every claim should be attributable.
2. **Operational fluency is the brand.** Use the language of the buyer's actual workflows — incidents, exceptions, escalations, SLAs, KYC queues. Specificity is what separates Toeshee from a relabeled BPO.
3. **Calm is credibility.** A low-drama buyer wants a low-drama partner. Restrained motion, generous whitespace, confident typography. No urgency tricks, no countdowns, no hype.
4. **Crypto-native without crypto-cosplay.** Signal domain depth through content and precision, not neon tokens or blockchain visual clichés.
5. **Real people, real operations.** Photography shows actual support operations (teams, screens, workflows), never synthetic or generic stock-smile imagery.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG conformance target. Follow good defaults: readable contrast for body text, keyboard-navigable menus and forms, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animations (already partially implemented), descriptive alt text on images.
